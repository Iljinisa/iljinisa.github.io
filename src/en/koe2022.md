

### Story related to the assignments

Funnydata is a software company that produces a study management system called Susi for the University of Helsinki. The application has encountered many problems, and the company has hired you to investigate the situation. The following is a description of Funnydata\'s operating methods.

The company has divided its software developers into three teams: the frontend team is responsible for the application\'s user interface, the backend team is responsible for the application logic running on the servers, and the DevOps team is responsible for server installation and maintenance. Funnydata develops applications in an agile manner using 3-month sprints. Each team has its own product owner, who decides what each team will focus on during the sprint.

At Funnydata, work is done entirely remotely in a modern way. Coders receive assignments written as user stories in the Jira system. Each team works on its own backlog. Coders are shielded from customers; only the product owner communicates with customers and end users. It sometimes takes a long time to complete the functionality visible to the end user, as the functionality (e.g., the open university course registration function currently under development) usually requires each team to complete their stories related to the function. The stories of different teams often have to be done in a specific order, e.g., it is very difficult for the frontend team to proceed if the backend team has not already completed its own story for the same functionality in the previous sprint. However, when a feature is finally \"ready\" in its entirety, it often turns out that the coders have misunderstood the user stories written in the Jira system, and the feature does not correspond to what the end user wants, or that the frontend and backend teams have implemented their own stories in a slightly incompatible way and the whole thing does not work. Quite often, however, the reason lies with the DevOps team: even though the functionality has been coded well, the DevOps team has not had time or been able to take the functionality into production. Sometimes, deployment only happens in a later sprint of the DevOps team. This is especially true in winter, when the enthusiastic snowboarders of the DevOps team are not fully focused on work.

The world of higher education is very hectic, and this often leads to Funnydata\'s CEO receiving angry feedback, which results in a large number of small bug tickets and feature requests that the teams are forced to complete in the middle of ongoing sprints.
